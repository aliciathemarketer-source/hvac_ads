BUSINESS MODEL
The two-phase architecture that powers IntellaBooks from lead generation through platform sales

IntellaBooks is a Texas-based B2B AI automation company built by founders Alicia Martin and Xavier Sneed. The platform delivers AI-driven voice qualification, automated SMS and email follow-up, CRM pipeline management, and appointment booking under the IntellaBooks brand. The go-to-market model operates in two structured phases, anchored on a single strategic principle: validate the system internally before selling it externally.

📡

Phase 1: Performance Lead Generation

IntellaBooks runs paid campaigns targeting homeowners across specific home service verticals. The AI system contacts every lead within 10 seconds of opt-in, qualifies the job, and packages the outcome. Those qualified leads are sold directly to local contractors in the same niche. Every campaign produces real CPL data, real conversion rates, and real proof that the funnel works before the platform is ever offered to the market.

🚀

Phase 2: Platform Sales to Contractors

The same contractors who bought leads in Phase 1 become the platform buyers in Phase 2. The conversation shifts from "here are leads" to "here is the system that generated them." Contractors receive a fully branded CRM, AI caller, and automated follow-up engine under their own name. The Phase 1 results are the proof of concept that makes this conversation easy to close.

Core Strategic Principle If the system cannot generate results for IntellaBooks, it cannot be sold to clients. Every lead generated, every CPL achieved, and every qualified appointment booked in Phase 1 becomes documented evidence that makes the Phase 2 platform offer undeniable. The lead business and the software business are one compounding flywheel.

TARGET AVATARS
Two distinct customer profiles: who sees the ads and who buys the outcome

The IntellaBooks model is two-sided. Understanding precisely who occupies each side is the foundation of every creative decision, every AI script line, and every sales conversation. Conflating the two is the single most common mistake in a model like this one. The ads speak to homeowners. The revenue comes from contractors. They are never addressed with the same message.

🏠

Avatar A: The Texas Homeowner

Homeowners aged 30 to 65 across DFW, Houston, San Antonio, and Austin experiencing an active HVAC issue. Their AC stopped cooling in July. Their heater failed in January. They are not comparing prices or reading reviews. They are searching for the first company that responds. Speed and trust are the only two variables that matter to them at that moment. The ad, the form, and the AI call are all designed for this person.

🔧

Avatar B: The HVAC Contractor

Owner-operated HVAC companies in Texas generating $300K to $3M annually. Operationally stretched during peak season. Losing jobs because follow-up is too slow or after-hours calls go unanswered. Skeptical of agencies because they have been burned before. They buy qualified leads in Phase 1 because the proof is immediate. They buy the platform in Phase 2 because the proof is already in their revenue.

Critical Distinction Every ad script, every hook, and every AI call in Phase 1 is written for Avatar A. The homeowner is the audience. The contractor is the customer. The platform sale targets Avatar B exclusively. These two messages must never appear in the same creative.

THE OFFER
Outcome-first offer statements for every audience at every phase, built to be undeniable

An offer is not a description of what the platform does. It is a specific, measurable promise of what the customer receives. The rule is absolute: every ad, every AI call, and every follow-up message leads with the outcome, not the mechanism. "We have AI voice agents" is a feature. "Get a licensed HVAC technician to your door fast without waiting on hold" is an offer. Every audience requires its own version of this statement.

The AI call replaced the landing page. The conversation is the conversion event. Make the offer undeniable before the homeowner ever thinks about the next company on the list.
🏠

Phase 1 Ad Offer: To the Homeowner

"Get a licensed local HVAC technician to your door fast. No hold music, no callbacks the next morning. Tell us what is happening and we will have someone contact you within minutes, day or night."

🔧

Phase 1 Lead Sale: To the Contractor

"We deliver AI-screened, phone-qualified HVAC homeowner leads in your Texas service area. Every lead has confirmed their issue, location, and availability before you ever talk to them. You close the job."

⚡

Phase 2 Platform Offer: To the Contractor

"The system that generated your leads is now available for your business. Every inbound lead gets contacted in 10 seconds and followed up automatically for a full year. No setters, no missed jobs, no manual work."

📈

Phase 2 Roofing and Solar Lead Sale

"We deliver free inspection-ready homeowner leads for roofing, and pre-qualified solar prospects screened for home ownership and qualifying energy use, before you ever pick up the phone."

Four Mechanisms That Power Every Ad and Call Script:

  • 10-Second AI Response. The AI calls every lead before they close the browser tab. In home services, the first company to respond wins the job. Always. Response time within 5 minutes improves conversion by up to 391% over a 30-minute response.
  • 100K Daily Call Intelligence. Platform-wide call volume across all home service categories identifies real buying intent patterns in real time, informing which leads are ready to close and which need nurture.
  • 365-Day Automated Follow-Up. The AI follows up with every unclosed lead through calls, SMS, and email for a full year. 37% of all closed customers come from leads older than 90 days. The follow-up engine captures what every competitor abandons.
  • Dead Lead Revival Engine. The CRM database is treated as an active asset, not an archive. AI re-engagement campaigns target contacts who did not convert on first contact with seasonal triggers and updated offers, converting pipeline into revenue months after the original opt-in.

PLATFORM STRATEGY
Meta and Google running in parallel from day one: the case for both, and how each serves a distinct role

The strongest Phase 1 strategy runs Meta and Google simultaneously from the first day of launch. They are not competing for the same audience. They are capturing different segments of the same homeowner population at different moments in their decision journey. Running both from the start produces faster data, broader audience coverage, and a more defensible lead volume for contractor partners.

📱

Meta: Facebook and Instagram

Role: Demand creation and volume generation.

Meta reaches homeowners before they are actively searching. The native lead form captures them within the platform environment where the algorithm is optimized for in-app conversion. Lower CPL than Google, higher volume, and the retargeting and lookalike audience infrastructure compounds over time. Particularly effective for reaching homeowners with seasonal HVAC concerns before they hit emergency status.

🔍

Google Responsive Search Ads

Role: High-intent demand capture.

Google RSA captures homeowners who are already in pain and actively searching for a solution. When someone searches "AC repair near me" or "HVAC company Houston," they are ready to book. Google RSA allows multiple headline and description combinations to be tested simultaneously, with the algorithm surfacing the highest-performing combinations automatically. Highest-intent leads, fastest close cycle, and the strongest single-keyword ROI in home services.

LANDING PAGE ROLE
A post-lead trust amplifier: sent after opt-in, not before it

The landing page serves one specific role in this funnel. It is delivered to the homeowner after they have submitted a form and the AI has attempted contact. Its function is to reduce the chance they call a competitor while waiting, confirm what happens next, and reinforce the decision they just made. Cold traffic does not land here. That is a structural decision, not a preference.

The landing page becomes dramatically more powerful when it appears after someone has already raised their hand. As a front-end conversion tool, it creates friction. As a post-lead confirmation, it creates confidence.
  • Section 1: VSL (45 to 60 seconds). Short video showing the matching process and demonstrating the AI in action. Reinforces the homeowner's decision by showing exactly what is happening behind the scenes after they submit. A full VSL script will be provided for production.
  • Section 2: What Happens Next. Three-step visual: submitted, matching in progress, tech will contact within minutes. This is the most important section for reducing no-shows. Uncertainty is the primary reason homeowners call a competitor after submitting.
  • Section 3: Trust Signals. Licensed and insured network confirmation, Texas-based technician coverage, average response time displayed prominently. Proof that the homeowner made the right call.
  • Section 4: Re-Submit CTA. A one-tap re-contact option for homeowners who are not sure their submission was received. Routes back into the CRM and re-triggers the AI qualification call without creating a duplicate record.
  • Section 5: FAQ. Three to five questions that address the most common reasons homeowners bail before the contractor calls: "Is there a fee to use this?" "Are techs in your network licensed?" "What if no one calls me back?" Eliminating these three objections at this stage has a direct impact on show rate.
  • Section 6: Direct Contact Option. A text or call button for homeowners who want to speak to a human immediately. Routes to the same qualification flow, just initiated by the homeowner rather than the AI outbound system.

8-WEEK EXECUTION PLAN
A structured launch roadmap from campaign architecture through Phase 2 preparation
Week 1
Campaign Architecture and Contractor Partner Confirmation

The first week is structural, not tactical. No ads run until the revenue outcome is confirmed. This means identifying and onboarding at least one HVAC contractor partner in DFW or Houston who is committed to purchasing qualified leads at the agreed price point. Simultaneously, the Meta Ads Manager account is structured with correct campaign architecture: separate ad sets for each of the three launch hook variations, conversion tracking verified end-to-end, and the audience pool seeding begins using broad homeowner targeting in the target Texas metro.

Week 2
Creative Production and Google RSA Build

UGC creators receive the full script pack with all 10 hook variations and filming instructions. Each hook is filmed as a separate standalone intro to the same body script, producing 10 interchangeable openings for editing. Google RSA campaigns are built with service-line segmentation from day one: separate campaigns for AC repair, heating repair, and HVAC maintenance, each with 8 to 10 headline variants and 3 to 4 description variants loaded. Static top-of-funnel Meta ads go live this week to begin building the initial audience pool before video is ready.

Week 3
Full Campaign Launch Across Both Platforms

Meta UGC video campaigns launch with three ad sets, each carrying a different hook variation. Google RSA campaigns go live simultaneously. Both platforms are in a learning phase for the first two weeks and must not be adjusted in this window. Any creative, targeting, audience, or budget change during weeks 3 and 4 resets the algorithm and destroys the data being collected. The discipline to hold steady during the learning phase is the single most common place campaigns fail to reach their potential. Daily monitoring covers CPL, CTR, form completion rate, AI contact rate, and qualification rate. Every outcome is logged in the IntellaBooks CRM dashboard.

Weeks 4 and 5
Optimization: Data-Led Creative Refinement

Both platforms exit their learning phases. On Meta, the lowest-performing ad set is cut and replaced with a creative variation built from the winning hook. A new hook test is introduced against the current control. On Google RSA, headline and description combinations that are underperforming by conversion rate are swapped out, and bid strategy is adjusted toward top-converting service-line campaigns. Every AI qualification call recording from the first two weeks is reviewed to identify the most frequent homeowner objections and confusion points. The AI script is updated to address the top three. First qualified leads are delivered to the contractor partner this week.

Week 6
Validation Review and Scale Decision

At the end of week 6, the funnel is either validated or under review. Validation requires: Meta CPL below $80, Google RSA CPL below $150, AI contact rate above 70%, and a minimum of 10 qualified leads delivered to the contractor partner with documented contractor feedback on lead quality. If validation criteria are met, daily budget scales to the next tier on both platforms. The documented CPL, contact rate, and qualification benchmarks are formalized as the Phase 1 playbook. This data is the asset that makes the Phase 2 platform sale and the Plumbing niche launch ready to begin.

Week 7
Cinematic Creative Deployment and Retargeting Activation

With a validated retargeting audience now exceeding 1,000 contacts, cinematic high-impact ads are deployed to the warm audience pool and lookalike segments built from it. Google LSA is added as a third channel specifically for emergency-intent HVAC searches, layering on top of the existing RSA campaigns. Budget is rebalanced toward the highest-performing channel based on six weeks of live data. Platform split is reviewed and adjusted weekly going forward.

Week 8
Plumbing Niche Launch and Phase 2 Contractor Outreach

Plumbing campaigns are launched using the validated HVAC funnel structure: same platform split, same creative framework, same AI qualification flow with issue-specific script adjustments. Plumbing runs in parallel with HVAC using separate campaign budgets and independent performance dashboards. Simultaneously, the existing HVAC contractor partner is approached with the Phase 2 platform conversation, using the 6-week documented performance data as the proof point. Outreach to roofing contractor partners in Texas begins, using the same documented results as the sales foundation for the Phase 2 niche expansion.
